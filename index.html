Academics

Msc. Computer Science

2013 - 2015
Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais

In this work, we tackle the problem of finding points of interest (keypoints) in RGB-D data in real time. We approach the problem by extracting and fusing simple features vectors from both information sources and feeding it to a decision tree classifier previously trained. Part of this work was recognized by winning the Best Paper Award at Iberoamerican Congress on Pattern Recognition 2015.

I had the opportunity to build a new keypoint detector from scratch by fusing intensity and range information. In addition to the already expected benefits of the information fusion (such as illumination and scale robustness) it has also shown very good performance even on common 2D intensity images scenarios such as translational motion, outperforming several state-of-the-art methods.
